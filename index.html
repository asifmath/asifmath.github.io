Mathematician

I am a mathematician specialized in the structural beauty of group theory. My research primarily navigates the interface of Combinatorial and Geometric Group Theory, with a specific focus on free groups and their subgroups.

I earned my Ph.D. from Savitribai Phule Pune University under the mentorships of Professor Hemant Bhate and Professor Dilip Sheth, where I explored the Galois coverings of Schreier graphs of self-similar groups. Following my doctoral work, I was honored to be a Fulbright-Nehru Postdoc Fellow at Texas A&M University, where I worked with Professor Rostislav Grigorchuk. During this time, my research expanded into the cogrowth of finitely generated subgroups of a free group and their multivariate analogs.

Beyond pure mathematics, I am captivated by the application of mathematical structures to Quantitative Biology and Finance. This interest was further honed during my tenure as an Assistant Professor at R A Podar College in Mumbai, where I bridged the gap between abstract theory and financial economics for my students.

Current Chapter

I have recently relocated to Frankfurt am Main, Germany. I am currently seeking new academic and research opportunities within the European mathematical community, where I can contribute my expertise in group theory, teaching, and interdisciplinary modeling.
