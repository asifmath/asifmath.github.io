Combinatorial & Geometric Group Theory

My work sits at the interface of combinatorial and geometric group theory, guided by two complementary perspectives:

  • Combinatorial: The study of generators, relations, and the action of $\mathrm{Aut}(F_m)$ on generating tuples, utilizing Whitehead moves as fundamental operations.
  • Geometric: Following Stallings, I employ the core graph $\Delta_H$ of a finitely generated subgroup $H$ to encode structure and measure complexity through vertex counts.
"Do subgroups that achieve minimal geometric complexity also tend to exhibit maximal cogrowth?"

Structural Complexity vs. Quantitative Growth

A central pillar of my research is connecting Whiteheadâ€™s classical theory with the quantitative measure of cogrowth ($\alpha_H$), as introduced by Grigorchuk.

While Whitehead moves allow us to detect and reduce the geometric complexity of a generating system, the cogrowth records the exponential growth rate of reduced words representing elements of $H$. My current motivation is to clarify whether structural simplifications (such as edge collapses in the core graph) systematically affect this quantitative growth.

Ultimately, this work aims to identify a potential duality between geometric simplicity and quantitative complexity, providing predictive control of cogrowth through structural criteria.
