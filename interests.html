The Tradition of Humanism

My worldview is rooted in the rich ethical and social traditions of Maharashtra. This lineage begins with the egalitarian spiritualism of Sant Dnyaneshwar and Sant Tukaram, and finds its political and social backbone in the visionary leadership of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. My perspective is further defined by the revolutionary reforms of Mahatma Phule, Rajarshi Shahu Maharaj, and Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, whose collective work dismantled barriers and championed human dignity.

I find a modern continuation of this great tradition in the legacy of Baba Amte and his family. Their philosophy of transforming social stigma into a "Forest of Bliss" (Anandwan) serves as a practical proof of how compassion and rigorous structure can solve the most complex human hardships.

"To me, the evolution from the verses of the Bhakti saints to the social justice of Ambedkar and the 'labor of love' at Anandwan represents a single, unbroken quest for a more equitable world."

Reading & Listening

Parallel to my work in mathematics, I have a deep affinity for Marathi Literature and Indian Music. Recently, I have enjoyed exploring cross-cultural narratives, such as P.L. Deshpande’s Jave Tyanchya Desha and Banu Mushtaq’s Heart Lamp.

I have a particular love for listening to Abhangas and Ghazals, finding in them a soul-stirring blend of poetry and melody. Currently, my favorite singer is Rahul Deshpande, whose artistry beautifully bridges the classical and contemporary traditions.
