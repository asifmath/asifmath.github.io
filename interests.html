Marathi Literature & Philosophy

My worldview is deeply shaped by the humanist traditions of Marathi literature. I find profound inspiration in the legacy of Baba Amte and his family, as chronicled in works like Samidha, Prakashvata, and Anandwan Prayogwan. Their philosophy—transforming social stigma into a "Forest of Bliss"—serves as a practical proof of how compassion and structure can solve the most complex human hardships.

"While mathematics defines the logical structure of the universe, literature defines the soul. I find a shared honesty in the rebellious verses of Namdev Dhasal and the lyrical depth of Kusumagraj."

Recently, I have enjoyed exploring cross-cultural narratives, such as P.L. Deshpande’s Jave Tyanchya Desha and Banu Mushtaq’s Heart Lamp.

Classical & Semi-Classical Music

Parallel to my work in mathematics, I have a deep affinity for Indian Classical and Semi-classical music. I am fascinated by how both disciplines are built upon rigorous, underlying structures—be it a Raga or an Axiom—that ultimately allow for infinite emotional and creative expression.
