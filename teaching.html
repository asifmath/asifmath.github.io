Philosophy & Approach

My teaching philosophy is centered on bridging the gap between abstract mathematical rigor and meaningful application. Whether navigating the complexities of group theory or the mechanics of financial models, I prioritize the "intuition and logic" behind the formulas over rote memorization.

"My goal is to empower students to perceive mathematics not as a rigid set of rules, but as a dynamic language capable of decoding and solving real-world complexities."

I am particularly interested in the potential for interdisciplinary frameworks that bring pure mathematics into dialogue with Quantitative Finance. I believe such a synthesis is essential for preparing students to navigate the analytical demands of both modern research and industry. Furthermore, I am currently exploring how these mathematical tools interface with the emerging field of Quantitative Biology.
